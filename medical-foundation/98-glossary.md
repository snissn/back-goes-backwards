<div class="glossary-section">
  <h1 id="ref-glossary">Glossary</h1>
  <p><strong>Alignment</strong> — The proper positioning of body structures that ensures optimal function and minimizes stress on joints and tissues.</p>
  <p><strong>Anterior Loading</strong> — A condition where prolonged sitting and poor posture shift strain to the front of the body, causing imbalances that lead to chronic pain and dysfunction, characterized by an overreliance on the body’s anterior structures, resulting in tissue compression and joint destabilization.</p>
  <p><strong>Autoimmune Diseases</strong> — Conditions where the immune system mistakenly attacks the body's own tissues, potentially linked to <strong>biomechanical dysfunction</strong>.</p>
  <p><strong>Autoimmune Disorders</strong> — Conditions where the immune system becomes dysregulated, often influenced by mechanical factors such as misalignment and chronic strain.</p>
  <p><strong>Axial Rotation</strong> — The twisting of the spine around its <strong>vertical axis</strong>.</p>
  <p><strong>Biological Organism</strong> — A living entity that consists of biological systems and processes essential for life.</p>
  <p><strong>Biomechanical Dysfunction</strong> — Disruptions in the body's mechanical systems that lead to pain, inefficiency, and systemic health issues.</p>
  <p><strong>Biomechanical-based Medicine</strong> — An approach to health that integrates biomechanics, physics, and engineering principles to understand and treat health conditions.</p>
  <p><strong>Bladder Meridian</strong> — A yang meridian that governs the back of the body and supports structural integrity.</p>
  <p><strong>Breathwork</strong> — Practices that utilize conscious breathing techniques to harmonize movement and energy flow.</p>
  <p><strong>Chong Meridian (Penetrating Vessel)</strong> — An extraordinary meridian in TCM that coordinates physical and energetic systems, connecting the spine with the pelvic and abdominal regions.</p>
  <p><strong>Chronic Conditions</strong> — Long-lasting health issues that often have complex origins, including biomechanical factors.</p>
  <p><strong>Chronic Fatigue Syndrome</strong> — A complex condition involving persistent fatigue, often exacerbated by <strong>mechanical dysfunction</strong> and systemic inefficiencies.</p>
  <p><strong>Chronic Fatigue Syndrome (CFS)</strong> — A debilitating condition marked by profound exhaustion, cognitive difficulties, and a host of physical symptoms.</p>
  <p><strong>Chronic Low Back Pain</strong> — A common condition linked to <strong>mechanical dysfunction</strong> in the lumbar spine, often exacerbated by misalignment and shear forces, and frequently stemming from <strong>anterior loading</strong> that places undue stress on the lumbar region.</p>
  <p><strong>Circulatory And Systemic Functions</strong> — Processes that can be disrupted by <strong>anterior loading</strong>, leading to issues such as <strong>venous insufficiency</strong> and impaired lymphatic flow.</p>
  <p><strong>Coupled Actions</strong> — Movements that involve multiple axes of motion occurring simultaneously.</p>
  <p><strong>Disc Herniation</strong> — A condition where the soft, gel-like center of a spinal disc pushes through its outer layer, potentially compressing nearby nerves and causing pain.</p>
  <p><strong>Du Meridian (Governing Vessel)</strong> — A major meridian in TCM that runs along the spine, governing yang energy and playing a vital role in maintaining health.</p>
  <p><strong>Dynamic Stability</strong> — The ability to maintain balance and <strong>alignment</strong> during movement while responding to changing forces.</p>
  <p><strong>Energy Inefficiency</strong> — The excessive energy expenditure required to maintain function due to biomechanical imbalances.</p>
  <p><strong>Ergonomic Adjustments</strong> — Modifications to workspaces or tools to enhance comfort and reduce strain on the body.</p>
  <p><strong>Facet Joints</strong> — Small joints in the spine that guide movement, limit excessive motion, and maintain vertebral <strong>alignment</strong>.</p>
  <p><strong>Fibromyalgia</strong> — A condition characterized by widespread pain and fatigue, often linked to mechanical inefficiencies and <strong>anterior loading</strong>.</p>
  <p><strong>Flexion-extension</strong> — Movements of the spine that involve bending forward and backward, affecting mechanical function and energetic flow through compression and elongation.</p>
  <p><strong>Force Distribution</strong> — The way forces are spread across the body's structures during movement, which is crucial for preventing localized stress and injury.</p>
  <p><strong>Gall Bladder Meridian</strong> — A meridian responsible for detoxification and emotional balance.</p>
  <p><strong>Holistic Health</strong> — An approach to health that considers the whole person, including physical, mental, and energetic aspects.</p>
  <p><strong>Holistic Strategy</strong> — An approach that considers the whole person, integrating multiple factors affecting health, including biomechanics.</p>
  <p><strong>Hormonal Imbalances</strong> — Disruptions in the body's hormonal systems that can be influenced by biomechanical health.</p>
  <p><strong>Inflammation</strong> — The body's response to injury or infection that can become chronic due to ongoing biomechanical stress.</p>
  <p><strong>Jalandhara Bandha (Throat Lock)</strong> — An energetic lock that aligns the cervical spine and regulates the flow of energy to the brain.</p>
  <p><strong>Joint Misalignment</strong> — A consequence of <strong>anterior loading</strong> that destabilizes key joints, leading to uneven wear on cartilage and ligaments.</p>
  <p><strong>Large Intestine Meridian</strong> — A meridian that extends from the arms into the head and torso, associated with respiration.</p>
  <p><strong>Lateral Axis</strong> — The axis of the spine that allows for side flexion, promoting flexibility and balanced posture while supporting stability in the side body.</p>
  <p><strong>Lateral Flexion</strong> — Bending the spine to one side, a motion guided by muscles like the quadratus lumborum and obliques.</p>
  <p><strong>Load Redistribution</strong> — The process of directing forces away from weaker structures to stronger ones to mitigate risks of injury and enhance mechanical performance.</p>
  <p><strong>Load Transmission</strong> — The process by which forces generated by movement and gravity are absorbed and redirected through the spine.</p>
  <p><strong>Localized Stress And Damage</strong> — Increased stress on specific areas of the body due to misalignment or improper <strong>force distribution</strong>, leading to injury or pain.</p>
  <p><strong>Lung Meridian</strong> — A meridian essential for breath and systemic balance.</p>
  <p><strong>Lymphatic Drainage</strong> — The process of clearing waste from the body, which can be optimized through proper <strong>alignment</strong> and movement.</p>
  <p><strong>Lymphedema</strong> — A condition characterized by swelling due to the buildup of lymphatic fluid from impaired drainage, which can be exacerbated by <strong>mechanical dysfunction</strong>.</p>
  <p><strong>Macrocosmic Orbit</strong> — A concept in TCM that describes the cyclical flow of <strong>Qi</strong> through the body, integrating inhalation and exhalation with spinal movement.</p>
  <p><strong>Mechanical Dysfunction</strong> — A failure in the body's mechanical systems that can lead to various health problems, often resulting from the body’s inability to distribute forces evenly across its structures, manifesting as <strong>anterior loading</strong> and contributing to <strong>chronic conditions</strong>.</p>
  <p><strong>Mechanical Foundation</strong> — The structural basis of the body that supports biological systems, including <strong>alignment</strong>, <strong>force distribution</strong>, and <strong>structural dynamics</strong>.</p>
  <p><strong>Mechanical System</strong> — A system that operates based on physical laws and principles, including the body's bones, muscles, and connective tissues.</p>
  <p><strong>Mechanical-based Medicine</strong> — A paradigm focusing on addressing chronic mechanical dysfunctions as a root cause of systemic health challenges, emphasizing the mechanical aspects of the body, including structure, <strong>alignment</strong>, and movement.</p>
  <p><strong>Mechanical-based Medicine (MBM)</strong> — A modern discipline that elucidates how mechanical dysfunctions, such as imbalances in posture and joint <strong>alignment</strong>, are central to many chronic and systemic health issues.</p>
  <p><strong>Meridians</strong> — Channels in the body through which <strong>Qi</strong> flows, connecting various organs and systems.</p>
  <p><strong>Movement Patterns</strong> — The habitual ways in which the body moves, which can contribute to <strong>biomechanical dysfunction</strong> if improper.</p>
  <p><strong>Mula Bandha (Root Lock)</strong> — An energetic lock at the pelvic floor that stabilizes the pelvis and grounds the body's energy.</p>
  <p><strong>Musculoskeletal Disorders</strong> — Conditions affecting the spine, joints, muscles, and connective tissues, often originating from mechanical dysfunctions.</p>
  <p><strong>Neurological Disorders</strong> — Conditions that affect the nervous system, potentially caused or exacerbated by <strong>biomechanical dysfunction</strong>.</p>
  <p><strong>Ordinary And Extraordinary Meridians</strong> — Categories of <strong>meridians</strong> in TCM, where ordinary <strong>meridians</strong> are more commonly referenced and extraordinary <strong>meridians</strong> have deeper connections to the body’s energy systems.</p>
  <p><strong>Osteoarthritis</strong> — A degenerative joint condition resulting from the breakdown of cartilage due to uneven joint loading and <strong>mechanical dysfunction</strong>.</p>
  <p><strong>Pelvic Congestion Syndrome</strong> — A condition characterized by chronic pelvic pain linked to <strong>venous insufficiency</strong> and exacerbated by <strong>mechanical dysfunction</strong>.</p>
  <p><strong>Peng</strong> — A buoyant quality in Tai Chi and other practices that represents outward expansion and stability.</p>
  <p><strong>Posterior Chain</strong> — A cohesive network of muscles, tendons, and connective tissues along the back of the body, including the glutes and hamstrings, crucial for maintaining structural integrity, proper <strong>alignment</strong>, and enabling dynamic movement while redistributing mechanical loads.</p>
  <p><strong>Posterior Loading</strong> — A condition where net mechanical force flows through the posterior side of the spine and associated muscles, redistributing forces away from the weaker anterior chain to enhance physical stability and systemic health, while engaging the <strong>posterior chain</strong> to restore mechanical balance.</p>
  <p><strong>Preventive Strategies</strong> — Approaches aimed at stopping health issues before they develop, particularly through biomechanical interventions.</p>
  <p><strong>Qi</strong> — The vital life force or energy that flows through the body, essential for maintaining health and vitality.</p>
  <p><strong>Rheumatoid Arthritis (RA)</strong> — A chronic autoimmune condition characterized by persistent joint <strong>inflammation</strong> and systemic fatigue.</p>
  <p><strong>Rotational Axis</strong> — The axis of the spine that allows for internal and external rotation, important for maintaining core stability and facilitating energy flow.</p>
  <p><strong>Sciatica</strong> — A condition characterized by radiating pain along the sciatic nerve, often caused by <strong>disc herniation</strong> or anterior pelvic tilt.</p>
  <p><strong>Spinal Alignment</strong> — The proper positioning of the spine that supports both mechanical function and energetic flow in the body.</p>
  <p><strong>Spinal Compression</strong> — A condition where the intervertebral discs become compressed due to excessive loading on anterior structures, leading to reduced shock absorption and spinal integrity.</p>
  <p><strong>Spinal Elongation</strong> — The process of lengthening the spine, often associated with improved posture and energy flow.</p>
  <p><strong>Spinal Realignment</strong> — Therapeutic interventions aimed at correcting misalignment in the spine to improve overall health.</p>
  <p><strong>Spinal Resilience</strong> — The ability of the spine to maintain <strong>alignment</strong>, functionality, and adaptability over time.</p>
  <p><strong>Spinal Stabilizers</strong> — Muscles that safeguard the integrity and mobility of the vertebral column, including the erector spinae, multifidus, and thoracolumbar fascia.</p>
  <p><strong>Spleen Meridian</strong> — A meridian that supports nourishment and internal energy distribution.</p>
  <p><strong>Stomach Meridian</strong> — A meridian associated with digestion, flowing along the legs and torso.</p>
  <p><strong>Strain Energy</strong> — The energy stored in a material under deformation, which can lead to structural integrity degradation if excessive.</p>
  <p><strong>Structural Dynamics</strong> — The behavior of the body's structures in response to forces, including how they adapt to movement and posture.</p>
  <p><strong>Symmetry</strong> — The balanced distribution of forces across the <strong>facet joints</strong>, crucial for spinal health.</p>
  <p><strong>Synthesis Of Motion</strong> — A framework that integrates insights from biomechanics and energy systems into a unified theory of health and movement.</p>
  <p><strong>Systemic Conditions</strong> — Health issues such as <strong>autoimmune disorders</strong> and <strong>hormonal imbalances</strong> that can be exacerbated by <strong>mechanical dysfunction</strong>.</p>
  <p><strong>Systemic Processes</strong> — Functions that affect the entire body, including circulation, nerve signaling, and immune responses.</p>
  <p><strong>Three-dimensional Motion</strong> — Movement that incorporates vertical, rotational, and lateral components to maintain health.</p>
  <p><strong>Three-dimensional Spinal Motion</strong> — The ability of the spine to move in three primary dimensions: <strong>flexion-extension</strong>, rotation, and <strong>lateral flexion</strong>.</p>
  <p><strong>Traditional Chinese Medicine (TCM)</strong> — An ancient system of healing that emphasizes the flow of <strong>Qi</strong> through <strong>meridians</strong> and the balance of yin and yang energies for optimal health, viewing health through the lens of pathways that carry <strong>Qi</strong> to sustain the body’s vitality.</p>
  <p><strong>Triple Burner Meridian</strong> — A meridian that harmonizes emotional balance and circulatory flow.</p>
  <p><strong>Uddiyana Bandha (Abdominal Lock)</strong> — An energetic lock that directs energy upward while decompressing the lumbar spine.</p>
  <p><strong>Ujjayi Breathing</strong> — A specific breathing technique used in yoga that emphasizes a steady, controlled breath to enhance physical <strong>alignment</strong> and energetic balance.</p>
  <p><strong>Venous Insufficiency</strong> — A condition where veins struggle to return blood to the heart, often due to compression or misalignment, characterized by impaired blood flow from the lower limbs back to the heart, frequently linked to anterior pelvic tilt and poor posture.</p>
  <p><strong>Venous Return</strong> — The flow of blood back to the heart, which can be improved by reducing compression in the pelvic and lumbar regions.</p>
  <p><strong>Vertical Axis</strong> — The axis of the spine that allows for flexion and extension, crucial for balance and stability, encompassing movements central to elongation.</p>
